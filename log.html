1. applyPositionConstraint inside PBD's aFixed branch was rotating the analytically-solved crank, corrupting the position of its far-end hole. Fix: guard the PBD application with motorConnectedIds — if a part is analytically solved, PBD does not touch it.

Exchange 34 USER ● critical bug

User reported: slotted strip with two blue ground joints (one at each end) moves/shifts when simulation runs, even though it should stay fixed.

Root cause: The PBD ground enforcement was translation-only — it pinned a single hole to its world position by adjusting x, y but never touched rotation. For parts with two ground joints at different holes, each enforcement pass pulled the part to a different position without ever locking the orientation. Over 60 iterations the part would translate but rotate freely, causing drift.

Fix in stepSimulation:

  • Added groundsByPart map: partId → [{holeIdx, x, y}] collecting ALL ground joints per part (the existing groundByPart only kept the last one).
  • Derived fullyFixed2 set: parts with 2+ entries in groundsByPart.
  • Added snapFullyFixed(): for each fully-fixed part, calls solveGroundedPart(part, g1.holeIdx, g1.x, g1.y, g2.holeIdx, g2.x, g2.y) — this solves BOTH translation and rotation simultaneously by treating one ground as the anchor and the other as the direction target.
  • Called snapFullyFixed() once at the start of each step (before motors/kinematic) and once after PBD iterations to eliminate any accumulated drift.
  • In the PBD ground-enforcement loop, added if (fullyFixed2.has(entry.partId)) continue — fully-fixed parts are no longer touched by the translation-only enforcer.
Observation

The pattern: translation-only constraints are sufficient for singly-grounded parts (rotation is determined by the pivot chain), but break for multiply-grounded parts where orientation must also be pinned. solveGroundedPart already existed and is the right primitive — it was just not being applied to the right parts.
